<Callout variant="danger" title="Sell what you are allowed to sell">
  The rules can change, so read them fresh before you list anything. The three that bind you: the Cfx.re Platform License Agreement (the PLA - the contract you agree to by running a server), the Tebex Acceptable Use Policy, and the Tebex Terms of Service. Add any publisher-specific restrictions on top. Do not resell assets you do not own. And take payment ONLY through Tebex - it is FiveM's one authorized monetization partner, so any other platform or payment provider is flat-out prohibited. Never collect card numbers yourself or accept side payments (PayPal, crypto, direct transfer) for server goods. Doing so breaks the PLA, throws away the transaction insurance Tebex gives you, and leaves you wide open to chargebacks and losing your account. This lesson is operational guidance, not legal advice or a substitute for the live policies.
</Callout>

## Run a compliant store

<StepGroup>
  <Step num={1} title="Publish a clear refund policy" outcome="Buyers and you both know the terms up front.">
    Tell buyers the terms before they pay. You decide the refund window and conditions, and digital goods are often non-refundable once delivered. One catch: a flat "no refunds" line does not hold up everywhere. Some regions give shoppers a cooling-off period, and a few keep limited rights even on digital goods unless the buyer waived them at checkout. So write the policy to respect that instead of pretending it does not exist. Bottom line: a clear written policy you can point to kills most arguments before they start.
  </Step>

  <Step num={2} title="Understand chargebacks" outcome="You know the difference between a refund and a chargeback.">
    Here is the difference in one line: a refund is you giving money back; a chargeback is the bank yanking it back over your head. A **refund** is money you choose to return to the buyer through Tebex. A **chargeback** is the buyer skipping you and asking their card provider (their bank or card network, called the "issuer") to force-reverse the charge - which usually slaps you with a fee on top of the lost sale. That kind of dispute is decided by the bank or network, not by you and not by Tebex. So watch the deadline: respond within the exact window shown in your Tebex creator panel (the dashboard at creator.tebex.io where you manage your store). Do not trust a generic number of days, because the card network sets that clock, not Tebex and not this course. Submit honest order, delivery, terms, and support records. The issuer decides the outcome, and even strong evidence never guarantees a win.
  </Step>

  <Step num={3} title="Keep records" outcome="You can prove delivery and terms in a dispute.">
    Save the receipts. Keep order records, delivery confirmation, and your published terms for every sale. When a chargeback hits, proof that the buyer accepted the terms and got the goods is the thing that saves you.
  </Step>

  <Step num={4} title="Stay inside platform and ecosystem rules" outcome="Your store and server are not at risk from what you sell.">
    The hard limits come from three places: the live Cfx.re Platform License Agreement, publisher restrictions, and the Tebex Acceptable Use Policy. Do not guess that something is fine just because you slapped a friendly label on it like cosmetic or supporter. Check the actual thing you are selling against the current policy pages before you sell it, write down the date you checked, and ask Tebex or Cfx support when the wording is fuzzy.
  </Step>
</StepGroup>

## Try it yourself

<Exercise>
  Write your refund policy in five lines before you take a single sale. One line each: the refund window (for example, "no refunds once a package is delivered, except as required by law"), what counts as delivery, how a buyer asks for help instead of charging back, the response time you promise, and the one sentence the buyer ticks to accept the terms at checkout. Keep it plain. Then hold it against the three boundaries in Step 4 and confirm nothing you sell crosses a Cfx.re or Tebex line. This little draft is the heart of your evidence when a chargeback lands. You submit it through Tebex, and Tebex passes it to the payment processor and the buyer's bank for you - you never contact the bank yourself.
</Exercise>
