<Callout variant="danger" title="Sell what you are allowed to sell">
  Monetization in the FiveM space is governed by the Cfx.re Platform License Agreement and by Tebex's Acceptable Use Policy. Three lines you do not cross: do not sell anything that gives a gameplay advantage (pay-to-win), do not sell in-game currency or items for real money in a way that affects balance, and do not resell escrowed scripts or other creators' assets. Tebex is also the only permitted payment route on Cfx.re; routing players to your own checkout is a PLA violation. Crossing any of these risks your store and your server key.
</Callout>

## Run a compliant store

<StepGroup>
  <Step num={1} title="Publish a clear refund policy" outcome="Buyers and you both know the terms up front.">
    State your refund window and conditions before purchase: digital goods are often non-refundable once delivered, and you decide and publish the policy. One caveat: a blanket "no refunds" line is not automatically enforceable everywhere. Some regions grant consumers a cooling-off period, and a few keep limited rights even on digital goods unless the buyer explicitly waived them at checkout. Write the policy to respect that rather than pretend it does not exist. A written policy you can point to still defuses most disputes.
  </Step>

  <Step num={2} title="Understand chargebacks" outcome="You know the difference between a refund and a chargeback.">
    A **refund** is you returning money voluntarily through Tebex. A **chargeback** is the buyer's bank reversing the payment, which carries fees and can hurt your standing with the processor. When a chargeback lands, Tebex passes you the dispute and a response window (usually a few days), and the bank decides who wins on the evidence. Reply with your published policy, the order record, and proof of delivery: timestamps showing the package was granted, the buyer accepting your terms at checkout, and any support thread where you offered help. Evidence that the buyer received exactly what they paid for is what reverses a dispute in your favor.
  </Step>

  <Step num={3} title="Keep records" outcome="You can prove delivery and terms in a dispute.">
    Keep order records, delivery confirmation, and your published terms. In a chargeback, evidence that the buyer accepted the terms and received the goods is what protects you.
  </Step>

  <Step num={4} title="Stay inside platform and ecosystem rules" outcome="Your store and server are not at risk from what you sell.">
    The Cfx.re Platform License Agreement and the Tebex Acceptable Use Policy set the hard limits. The concrete boundaries you have to respect: no selling a gameplay advantage (a perk that makes a paying player stronger than a non-paying one is pay-to-win), no selling in-game currency or items for real money in a way that affects balance, no reselling escrowed scripts or other creators' assets, and no payment route other than Tebex. Cosmetic-only perks, priority queue, and supporter roles that do not change gameplay are the safe lane. When unsure whether a perk is allowed, treat it as not allowed until you confirm it against the policy pages below.
  </Step>
</StepGroup>

## Try it yourself

<Exercise>
  Draft your store's refund policy in five lines, before you ever take a sale. Write one line for each: the refund window (for example, "no refunds once a package is delivered, except as required by law"), what counts as delivery, how a buyer requests help instead of charging back, the response time you commit to, and the one sentence the buyer ticks to accept the terms at checkout. Keep it plain. Then read it against the three boundaries in Step 4 and confirm nothing you sell crosses a Cfx.re or Tebex line. This five-line draft is the exact text you point a bank to when a chargeback lands.
</Exercise>
