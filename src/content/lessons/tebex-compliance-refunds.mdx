## Run a compliant store

<StepGroup>
  <Step num={1} title="Publish a clear refund policy" outcome="Buyers and you both know the terms up front.">
    State your refund window and conditions before purchase: digital goods are often non-refundable once delivered, and you decide and publish the policy. One caveat: a blanket "no refunds" line is not automatically enforceable everywhere. Some regions grant consumers a cooling-off period, and a few keep limited rights even on digital goods unless the buyer explicitly waived them at checkout. Write the policy to respect that rather than pretend it does not exist. A written policy you can point to still defuses most disputes.
  </Step>

  <Step num={2} title="Understand chargebacks" outcome="You know the difference between a refund and a chargeback.">
    A **refund** is money you voluntarily return to the buyer through Tebex. A **chargeback** is different: the buyer skips you entirely and asks their card provider (their bank or card network -- the "issuer") to forcibly reverse the charge, which can cost you a fee on top of the lost sale. A payment dispute raised this way is decided by that bank or network, not by you or Tebex. Use the exact response deadline shown in your Tebex creator panel (the management dashboard at creator.tebex.io, where you manage the store you set up in the Tebex setup lesson) -- do not rely on a generic number of days, because the dispute window is set by the card network, not by Tebex or by this course. Submit truthful order, delivery, terms, and support records. The issuing bank or payment network decides the outcome, and evidence never guarantees a win.
  </Step>

  <Step num={3} title="Keep records" outcome="You can prove delivery and terms in a dispute.">
    Keep order records, delivery confirmation, and your published terms. In a chargeback, evidence that the buyer accepted the terms and received the goods is what protects you.
  </Step>

  <Step num={4} title="Stay inside platform and ecosystem rules" outcome="Your store and server are not at risk from what you sell.">
    The live Cfx.re agreement, publisher restrictions, and Tebex AUP set the hard limits. Do not infer compliance from labels such as cosmetic, supporter, or pay-to-win. Compare the exact deliverable against the current policy pages before sale, keep a dated record of that review, and ask Tebex/Cfx support when the wording is unclear.
  </Step>
</StepGroup>

## Try it yourself

<Exercise>
  Draft your store's refund policy in five lines, before you ever take a sale. Write one line for each: the refund window (for example, "no refunds once a package is delivered, except as required by law"), what counts as delivery, how a buyer requests help instead of charging back, the response time you commit to, and the one sentence the buyer ticks to accept the terms at checkout. Keep it plain. Then read it against the three boundaries in Step 4 and confirm nothing you sell crosses a Cfx.re or Tebex line. This five-line draft is the core of the evidence you submit through Tebex when a chargeback lands -- Tebex forwards it to the payment processor and the buyer's bank, so you do not contact the bank yourself.
</Exercise>
