## Add it without breaking the server

<StepGroup>
  <Step num={1} title="Understand what gets streamed" outcome="You know which files cost performance.">
    Clothing/EUP packs ship as streamed assets (drawables, textures, and metadata). The client downloads them on join, so large packs mean slow joins and bigger memory use. Size discipline is the whole game here.
  </Step>

  <Step num={2} title="Add the pack as a resource" outcome="The pack starts and the items appear in your clothing menu.">
    Place the pack in resources, ensure it, and wire it to your clothing/menu resource per its docs. Restart and confirm the items show up.
  </Step>

  <Step num={3} title="Optimize: only ship what you use" outcome="Join times and memory stay reasonable.">
    Cut variants you will never use, keep textures at sane resolutions, and avoid stacking ten overlapping packs. Every megabyte is downloaded by every player on join.
  </Step>

  <Step num={4} title="Document the license" outcome="You can prove you are allowed to use each asset.">
    Keep a note of where each pack came from and its license terms. If you cannot point to a license, do not ship it.
  </Step>
</StepGroup>
