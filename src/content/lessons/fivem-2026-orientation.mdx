<Callout variant="fivem" title="The one idea to hold onto">
  A FiveM server is four layers: the **platform** (FiveM + txAdmin), the **language** (Lua), the **framework** (ESX, QBCore, or Qbox), and the **data** (a MariaDB database your scripts talk to through oxmysql). Every resource you ever install plugs into those four layers. Learn the layers, not the scripts.
</Callout>

## Build it

You are building your bearings, not a resource yet. Each step ends with something concrete: a bookmark or a sentence you can say out loud.

<StepGroup>
  <Step num={1} title="Name the platform layer" outcome="You can say what FiveM and txAdmin each do, and you have the portal bookmarked.">
    **FiveM** is the multiplayer platform built on top of GTA V by Cfx.re. **txAdmin** is the web panel that ships *inside* FXServer, the FiveM server process itself, and lets you start, stop, and watch it from your browser. You do not install txAdmin separately; it is already there the moment you run FXServer. Your free server license key comes from the **Cfx.re Portal**, not from any "keymaster" page or Discord link.

    Bookmark the portal now. You will need a free key before your server will boot.

    <TechLink id="cfxportal" />
  </Step>

  <Step num={2} title="Name the language layer" outcome="You know scripts are Lua 5.4, and you have the two reference sites bookmarked.">
    Most beginner resources in this course are written in **Lua 5.4**. FiveM also supports JavaScript and C#, so a downloaded resource may use one of those runtimes. You do not need to memorize functions. You need to know how to look up the built-in game functions, called **natives**. Bookmark the official docs and the natives reference; you will live in these.

    <TechLinks ids={["lua", "fivem-docs", "natives"]} />
  </Step>

  <Step num={3} title="Pick a framework lane" outcome="You can explain the difference between ESX, QBCore, and Qbox, and which one to start on in 2026.">
    A **framework** answers the boring questions for every script: who is this player, how much money do they have, what job are they on. You install one framework and your scripts build on it. Three matter:

    - **ESX Legacy** has the largest script catalog and the most copy-paste tutorials, especially in the EU.
    - **QBCore** remains widely used and has its own official documentation and repository.
    - **Qbox** began as a QBCore fork, describes itself as its successor, integrates the ox resources, and offers an official txAdmin recipe.

    For a brand-new server, compare the official recipes and the compatibility requirements of the scripts you plan to use. This course demonstrates ESX Legacy and Qbox most often, but there is no single correct framework for every server.

    <TechLinks ids={["esx", "qbox", "qbcore"]} />
  </Step>

  <Step num={4} title="Name the library layer" outcome="You can explain what ox_lib and friends give you, and why nearly every modern resource depends on them.">
    A **framework** is the base; a **library** is a shared toolbox that many resources reuse. **Overextended (ox)** maintains the 2026 standard set: `ox_lib` (menus, notifications, callbacks), `ox_inventory`, `ox_target`, and `oxmysql` (the database connector). When a script's install guide says "dependency: ox_lib", this is what it means.

    <TechLinks ids={["ox", "oxmysql"]} />
  </Step>

  <Step num={5} title="Name the data layer" outcome="You can explain the database vs the client vs the connector, in one sentence each.">
    Player money, vehicles, jobs, and inventory survive a restart because they live in a **database**. Three names, three jobs:

    - **MariaDB** is the database engine that stores the data.
    - **HeidiSQL** is the visual window you browse and edit that data through.
    - **oxmysql** is the FiveM resource your scripts use to talk to MariaDB safely.

    <TechLinks ids={["mariadb", "heidisql", "oxmysql"]} />

    <ExpectedOutput>
      You can now finish these sentences without looking:
      - "FiveM is the platform; txAdmin is the panel that runs it."
      - "Scripts are written in Lua; I look functions up in the natives docs."
      - "A framework is ESX, QBCore, or Qbox; a library is ox_lib and friends."
      - "Data lives in MariaDB; HeidiSQL views it; oxmysql connects to it."
    </ExpectedOutput>
  </Step>
</StepGroup>
