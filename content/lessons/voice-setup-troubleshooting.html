Voice chat setup and troubleshooting

Voice is the hardest thing to get right in FiveM. When it works nobody notices. When it breaks, your server is a ghost town. This lesson covers setup, the four things that break voice, and how to fix each one in under five minutes.

You'll ship
Working proximity voice chat with 3D audio, radio channels, and no popping or echo, running on pma-voice or saltychat.
Time
About 30 minutes to set up, then 5 minutes per problem when it breaks.
You'll learn
How voice tokens work, why UDP ports break voice, and the four debug steps that fix 95% of voice issues.
Prereqs
Your FiveM server starts and players can join. You have access to your firewall or hosting panel.
In 3 bullets

By the end of this lesson your voice chat will do this:

  • Players hear each other when close. Volume fades with distance. Left/right direction is correct.
  • Radio channels work separately from proximity. Players on channel 1 do not hear channel 2.
  • When voice breaks — and it will — you know the four things to check, in order, without guessing.

Why it matters

A roleplay server without voice is a text chat room. Players join, realize they cannot talk, and leave within 30 seconds. Voice is the first thing they test and the last thing they forgive.

Vocabulary

UDP
The network protocol voice uses. Faster than TCP, but some hosts block it.
Example: if UDP port 30122 is closed, voice will not work.
Token
A free key that identifies your server to the voice provider.
Example: pma-voice gives you a token at their website.
Proximity
How close you must be to hear someone. Usually three ranges: whisper, normal, shout.
Example: normal voice has a range of about 15 meters.

Minimal example

Expected output: F8 console shows [pma-voice] Connected to voice server when a player joins. Two players standing near each other can hear each other.

Checklist

  1. Clone pma-voice into your resources folder.
  2. Get a free token from the pma-voice website.
  3. Add endpoint_add_udp "0.0.0.0:30122" to server.cfg.
  4. Open UDP port 30122 in your firewall or hosting panel.
  5. Add ensure pma-voice to server.cfg.
  6. Have two players join and stand near each other. Confirm they can hear each other.

Exercise

Change the normal voice distance from 15 meters to 25 meters. Restart the resource. Walk 20 meters away from another player and confirm you can still hear them. Change it back to 15 and confirm the range shrinks.

Expected output: At 25m range, voice is audible at 20 meters. At 15m range, voice cuts out beyond 15 meters.

Common failures

  • Voice not connecting (F8 shows "disconnected") — the UDP port is closed. Check your firewall and hosting panel.
  • Players can hear each other but it echoes — one player has their speakers loud enough that their microphone picks up the game audio. Tell them to use headphones.
  • Voice works for some players but not others — the affected players have their voice chat disabled in FiveM settings. Tell them to press ESC > Settings > Voice > Enable.
  • pma-voice starts but nobody can talk — the token is invalid or expired. Get a new token from the pma-voice website.

Refactor

Move the voice token to a voice_token.cfg file loaded via exec, so you never accidentally commit it to a public repository.

Cheat sheet

Voice debug: the four things to check, in order

  • 1. F8 console. Does it say "Connected to voice server" or "Disconnected"? If disconnected, the problem is the UDP port or the token.
  • 2. UDP port. Run netstat -tuln | grep 30122 on the server. If the port is not listening, your endpoint_add_udp line is wrong or the port is blocked.
  • 3. Token. Did you copy the full token? Did it expire? Get a new one and paste it into the config.
  • 4. Client settings. Have the player press ESC > Settings > Voice. Is "Voice Chat" set to "Enabled"? Is their microphone selected?

The big idea, in one minute

FiveM voice is not magic. It is a separate UDP connection between each player and a voice server. The game server (your Lua code) tells the voice server who is near whom. The voice server mixes the audio and sends it back. If any link in that chain is broken — the UDP port, the token, the client settings — voice goes silent. The fix is always one of four things.

Think of a walkie-talkie system.

Your game server is the dispatcher. The voice server is the radio tower. Each player is a walkie-talkie. The dispatcher tells the tower "player A and player B are 10 meters apart, let them talk." The tower opens a channel between A and B. If the dispatcher cannot reach the tower (UDP port closed), or the tower does not recognize the dispatcher (bad token), or the walkie-talkie's volume knob is at zero (voice disabled in settings) — no sound. Four links. Four fixes.

Run the four checks in order. Do not guess. Do not reinstall. Check F8, check the port, check the token, check the client. One of those four is always the answer.

Before you start

If any box is unchecked, you will struggle with voice.

Step 1

Install pma-voice

Clone the resource, get a token, configure it.

  1. Go to https://github.com/AvarianKnight/pma-voice and clone or download the latest release.
  2. Place the folder in resources/pma-voice.
  3. Visit https://voice.pma-voice.net and create a free account. Generate a token.
  4. Open resources/pma-voice/config/config.lua. Paste your token where it says "default".
  5. Do not change anything else in the config yet. The defaults work for most servers.
Step 2

Open the UDP port

Voice needs its own UDP port. If the port is closed, voice will not connect.

  1. Open server.cfg. Add the line: endpoint_add_udp "0.0.0.0:30122".
  2. If you use a hosting panel (Zap, Iceline, etc.), open UDP port 30122 in the panel's firewall settings.
  3. If you run on a VPS, run: sudo ufw allow 30122/udp (UFW) or sudo iptables -A INPUT -p udp --dport 30122 -j ACCEPT (iptables).
  4. Add ensure pma-voice to server.cfg.
  5. Start the server. In the server console, you should see no errors from pma-voice.
Step 3

Test with two players

Voice is a two-player feature. You cannot test it alone.

  1. Have a friend join your server. Both of you should press F8 and look for [pma-voice] Connected to voice server.
  2. Stand within 15 meters of each other in-game. One person talks. The other listens.
  3. If you can hear each other, voice works. Move 20 meters apart — the voice should fade.
  4. If you cannot hear each other, run the four debug checks from the cheat sheet, in order.
  5. Fix whatever check fails. Test again. Repeat until voice works.
Recap

Three tests, in order

Run each test with two players. Do not test voice alone.

  1. Test 1, proximity works.
    • Action: two players stand 5 meters apart. One talks. Then walk 20 meters apart.
    • Expected: voice is clear at 5m, quiet at 15m, silent at 20m.
    • How to verify: the F8 console shows no voice errors during the test.
  2. Test 2, radio channels isolate.
    • Action: player A joins radio channel 1. Player B joins radio channel 2. Both talk.
    • Expected: A does not hear B's radio. B does not hear A's radio. Proximity voice still works normally.
    • How to verify: the radio UI shows the correct channel. Voice is only heard on the matching channel.
  3. Test 3, reconnect preserves voice.
    • Action: player A disconnects and reconnects. Both players test voice again.
    • Expected: voice works immediately after reconnect without any manual action.
    • How to verify: F8 shows "Connected to voice server" on reconnect without delay.
Why does voice need UDP instead of TCP?

TCP retransmits lost packets, which adds delay. In voice chat, a lost packet means a tiny gap in audio that you do not notice. A delayed packet arriving 500ms late is worse than a lost packet — it creates echo and jitter. UDP drops the packet instead of waiting. The result is real-time audio with no delay.

The rules you just followed

  • Voice needs a separate UDP port. TCP (the game connection) is not enough.
  • The F8 console is your first debug tool. "Connected" or "Disconnected" tells you where to look.
  • Tokens are free but must be valid. An expired token silences your entire server.
  • Test with two players. Voice requires at least one speaker and one listener.
  • Headphones prevent echo. One player with speakers will ruin voice for everyone.

What you learned

  • Voice requires a separate UDP port opened in both server.cfg and your firewall.
  • pma-voice uses a free token to identify your server. An invalid or expired token breaks voice.
  • The four debug checks: F8 console, UDP port, token validity, client voice settings.
  • Echo is always caused by a player using speakers instead of headphones.
  • Test voice with two real players. One person cannot verify voice works.

Five-minute exercise

Break voice on purpose: remove the UDP endpoint line from server.cfg. Restart the server. Observe what the F8 console says. Then add the line back, restart, and confirm voice returns. You just learned to recognize a port issue without anyone telling you.

Expected output

Hint

After removing endpoint_add_udp, the voice resource starts but cannot open the UDP listener. The F8 console will show a connection failure. This is the exact error your players will see when the port is blocked. Knowing this error message saves you from checking the wrong things.
