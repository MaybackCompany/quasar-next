Common failures

  • player not online during refund means the item revocation cannot run. Queue the revocation for next login.
  • refund webhook never fires means the Tebex webhook URL is not configured for the refund event type.
  • items not fully removed means partial grants (cash + vehicle + tag) need individual revocation calls for each type.
  • chargeback without notice means you did not check the Tebex disputes dashboard regularly. Set a weekly calendar reminder.

Refactor

Cheat sheet

Refund compliance rules

  • Deliver fast. Items must arrive within 60 seconds or Tebex considers it a failed delivery.
  • Display a policy. Your store page must show refund terms. Link it in the Tebex store settings.
  • Cooperate on chargebacks. When a bank disputes a charge, provide transaction logs within 48 hours.

The big idea, in one minute

A refund is not a punishment — it is a transaction in reverse. When money flows backward, items must flow backward too. If you treat a refund as just "delete the payment record," the player keeps free items and your economy breaks. Every refund must be a mirror image of the original purchase: remove what was granted, log what was removed, and leave a clear audit trail.

Think of a store return counter.

The customer comes back with the item and the receipt. The clerk checks the receipt (transaction ID), takes the item back (revocation), and issues the refund. If the customer walks in without the item, the clerk says no. If the receipt is fake, the clerk says no. Your refund handler is the clerk.

Bad way and good way, side by side

Most beginners ignore refunds entirely, or they just delete the database row. Both are dangerous.

Don't do this

The refund is ignored or handled silently. Items stay with the player.

The player keeps everything. The database says they never paid. You have no record of why the refund happened. If the bank asks for evidence, you have nothing.

Do this

Revoke items, log the reason, and write a permanent audit entry.

Items are revoked. The audit trail is permanent. Offline players are queued for revocation on next login. The payment record stays for compliance.

Step 1

Write the refund policy

Before code, write the words your customers will read.

  1. Open your Tebex store dashboard and go to Store Settings → Refund Policy.
  2. Write a short policy: "Refunds within 7 days if items were not delivered. No refunds for in-game bans or voluntary chargebacks."
  3. Save the policy. It must be visible on your store page.
  4. Copy the policy text into a refund_policy.txt file in your resource for reference during disputes.
Step 2

Create the refund handler

The handler listens for Tebex refund events and reverses the grant.

  1. Create resources/qu_tebex/refunds.lua.
  2. Paste the safe version from the good example above.
  3. Implement revokePackage(playerId, packageId) by removing cash, vehicles, and tags granted in the original purchase.
  4. Add refunds.lua to fxmanifest.lua under server_scripts.
Step 3

Create the support template

A good support template collects the right info before you read the ticket.

  1. Create a Discord channel or forum for support tickets.
  2. Pin a template that asks: (a) Tebex transaction ID, (b) FiveM player name, (c) what they bought, (d) what went wrong.
  3. Set a bot auto-reply that links the template if the user posts without filling it.
  4. The template alone resolves 80% of tickets because most problems are self-inflicted and the user realizes it while filling the form.
Recap

Three tests, in order

Run each test and verify the output before moving on.

  1. Test 1, the happy path refund.
    • Action: simulate a refund webhook for a recent purchase in sandbox mode.
    • Expected: items are removed from the player's inventory or queued for removal.
    • How to verify: console prints [QUIET][refund] with the transaction ID.
  2. Test 2, offline player refund.
    • Action: simulate a refund for a player who is not on the server.
    • Expected: the revocation is queued in the pending_revocations table.
    • How to verify: the table has a new row. No console errors about nil player.
  3. Test 3, double refund.
    • Action: send the same refund webhook twice.
    • Expected: the second refund is ignored because the payment is already marked refunded.
    • How to verify: console prints only one refund line. The audit log has only one entry for that transaction.
Why queue revocations instead of skipping offline players?

Because a player who logs off right after a refund would keep the items forever. The queue guarantees the revocation runs on their next login.

The rules you just followed

  • Every refund removes the exact items granted by the original purchase.
  • Payment records are updated, never deleted — compliance requires an audit trail.
  • Offline players are queued for revocation, not ignored.
  • A visible refund policy is required by Tebex's terms of service.
  • Support templates collect the transaction ID before you spend time investigating.
