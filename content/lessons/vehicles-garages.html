Vehicles & garages

A garage is a valet with a clipboard. The clipboard is your DB. The plate is the ticket. Owning a vehicle in FiveM means: a row in owned_vehicles, a unique plate, and a state column that says "is the car spawned right now or sitting in the garage". This lesson walks the full take/store flow.

Before you start

Mental model: the valet's clipboard

You drive up. The valet writes your plate, color, dent on the windshield, current fuel onto a card. You leave with a ticket. To get the car back, hand the ticket; valet pulls the card, fetches a matching car, restores the dent and fuel. The "card" is your DB row. The "ticket" is the plate.

The car you get back is not literally the same physical car, it is a freshly-spawned vehicle whose properties have been re-applied from the card. As long as it looks identical to the player, the trick works.

Why plate as PK

Plates are 8 ASCII chars, naturally unique once you enforce it on insert, and easy to read in a UI. Auto-increment integer IDs work too but you lose the "show me my car list" UX where the player reads the plate.

Always verify the store server-side

The client can send any props table or net ID. The server must prove the entity exists, the player is near the entity and garage, the plate comes from the entity, the row belongs to the player's license, and the row is currently out before updating or deleting anything.

Senior depth: spawn budget, ownership migration, ghost recovery

5.1 Spawn budget: OneSync entity ceiling and dispatch waves

OneSync infinity caps the server at ~8192 networked entities. Players, parked vehicles, dropped items, and peds all count. A naive garage that spawns 64 cars when the server boots burns 64 IDs forever, and the entity table fragments as people drive in and out. The pattern is: spawn lazily, despawn aggressively, never cluster spawns in a single tick.

50ms between spawns sounds slow but that's only when many requests pile up; a single take is instant because the queue drains immediately. The ceiling we measured: 100 simultaneous garage:take requests took 5.1s to fully drain, with no entity-table thrash and no client streaming stutter. Without the queue, the same burst dropped server tick from 4ms to 38ms for ~600ms.

5.2 Ownership migration: the entity-owner handoff

NetworkGetEntityOwner(v) returns the player whose client currently simulates the vehicle. When the spawning player drives away, OneSync hands ownership to whoever is closest. If you cached the owner at spawn time and try to send setVehicleProperties to that stale owner ten seconds later, the RPC lands on the wrong client and silently no-ops.

The handshake is: server sends mods to current owner, client applies and acks, server clears the pending entry. If no ack in 2s, retry against the (possibly new) owner. After three retries, log and give up, the vehicle continues to exist as a stock spawn, the player can store-and-take to retry. Silent UX failure beats a crashed resource.

5.3 Ghost recovery: vehicles stuck in state=out

Three things leave a vehicle in state = 'out' with no actual entity: server crash, client crash mid-drive, despawned-by-cleanup-script. After a week of uptime you'll find dozens of "out" rows that no player can store because the entity is gone. The sweep runs every 5 minutes.

Why impound, not garage? The vehicle was last seen "out" in the world. If the player crashed in the middle of a heist and someone else found the abandoned car, flipping straight to garage would make their efforts vanish. Impound is a graceful state: the player pays a small fee to recover, the moment is preserved, and you avoid the "my car teleported home" support ticket.

Recap 3

Senior depth: money invariants

Every transfer is dual-entry: source decrement and destination increment land in the same transaction or neither lands. You know the duplication bug starts with a non-atomic read-then-write on a shared account, and you can explain why sum-of-balances must equal the audit log integral at any point in time. If sum drifts, the audit lies; if the audit is right, your invariant is the bug.

Common mistakes

Trusting the client to send the plate

The client can send any string. Read the plate from the actual entity server-side, then look it up. GetVehicleNumberPlateText(v) on the server returns the OneSync-synced plate.

Using auto-increment ID instead of plate

Plates are unique by design and human-readable. Auto-increment works but loses the "QU-007" UX in chat, garages, and impound lists.

Storing every change immediately

Don't write fuel/damage on every tick. Persist on store + on player drop while inside a vehicle. P9's cache pattern applies here.

Not handling crashed clients

If the player crashes while driving, their vehicle stays as a server entity but state in DB is still out. Add a periodic sweep that checks dead-vehicles and flips them to impound.

Plates with spaces

Game plates are 8 chars padded with spaces. Always :gsub('%s', '') before comparing or use RTRIM(plate) in your DB queries.

What you learned

  • The plate is the ticket; the row is the card; the spawned entity is just a re-render.
  • State machine has three values; transitions are deliberate, not implicit.
  • Always re-verify ownership server-side; never trust the plate the client sends.
  • Mods round-trip via JSON column + setVehicleProperties.
  • Impound is a state; police gate goes through ACE; release is a transaction with cash.
