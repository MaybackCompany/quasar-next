Support workflow for paid and premium scripts

By the end of this lesson you will have a support workflow that triages tickets into three buckets (self-serve, you-fix, their-fix), responds to paying customers within 24 hours, and protects your development time from becoming free consulting.

You'll ship
A Discord-based support system with a ticket bot, a triage script, and response templates that solve 70% of issues before you read the ticket.
Time
About 30 minutes if you go slow.
You'll learn
How to set boundaries between paid support and custom development, how to write templates that extract the right information, and how to track support load so you know when to raise prices.
Prereqs
You sell or plan to sell a paid FiveM script. That is enough.
In 3 bullets

You will build a support workflow that does this:

  • Route every ticket through a bot that asks for the transaction ID, script version, and error message before a human sees it.
  • Apply the three-bucket triage: self-serve (point to docs), your-fix (bug you own), and their-fix (misconfiguration on their server).
  • Track resolution time per ticket so you know when support load exceeds what your price covers.

Why it matters

Untriaged support is a time sink that scales with every sale. A workflow makes support scale with templates, not with your hours.

Vocabulary

Triage
Sorting tickets into categories based on who is responsible for the fix.
Example: a config typo is their-fix, a nil error on startup is your-fix.
SLA
Service Level Agreement — the promised response time for support.
Example: "All tickets responded to within 24 hours, resolved within 72."
Ticket Template
A form that captures required information before the conversation starts.
Example: "Transaction ID: ___ Version: ___ Error message: ___".

Common failures

  • every ticket becomes custom development means you are not enforcing the boundary between "the script is broken" and "can you change the script to do X."
  • customer cannot find their transaction ID means your delivery email or Tebex page does not display it prominently. Add it to the welcome message.
  • support takes 4 hours per day means your documentation is missing the answers to the top 5 questions. Write those docs.
  • angry customer in public channel means you do not have a rule that support happens in tickets, not in #general. Enforce it kindly.

Refactor

Move the triage patterns into a JSON config file so you can add new patterns without editing Lua code. The triage function reads the config and matches against it.

Cheat sheet

Support response templates

  • Self-serve: "This is covered in the docs at [link]. Let me know if that page is unclear and I will improve it."
  • Their-fix: "This looks like a configuration issue on your server. Check [config section]. If it still fails after that, reopen with your server.cfg excerpt."
  • Your-fix: "Confirmed — this is a bug. I have opened an internal ticket. Expect a fix in the next release. Workaround in the meantime: [workaround]."

The big idea, in one minute

Support is not free. Every minute you spend answering "where do I put the file" is a minute you are not building the next feature that brings in new sales. A workflow protects your time by forcing every ticket through a filter: did they read the docs? Did they provide the transaction ID? Is this a bug or a feature request? Most tickets fail the filter, and a good template resolves them without you typing a single custom reply.

Think of a doctor's office.

The patient does not walk directly into the operating room. They check in at the front desk (ticket template). A nurse takes their vitals and asks what is wrong (triage). Only then does the doctor see them. Your support workflow is the front desk and the nurse. You are the doctor. You only see the cases that need you.

Bad way and good way, side by side

Most solo developers answer every support ping in DMs, at all hours, with no boundaries. That is a fast track to burnout.

Don't do this

Answer every DM. No template. No triage. No boundaries.

No transaction ID means you cannot verify they even bought the script. No error message means you are guessing. No boundaries means you are on call 24/7 for $15.

Do this

Ticket template → triage → bucket response. Boundaries enforced.

The template collects everything you need before you open the ticket. The bot triages it. You open the ticket, see all the info, and respond in 2 minutes instead of 45.

Step 1

Create the support channel and template

A Discord forum channel with a pinned template is the smallest support system that works.

  1. In your Discord server, create a Forum Channel named "Support".
  2. Set the post guidelines to the template from the good example above.
  3. Add tags: "bug", "config", "question", "resolved".
  4. Create a separate #feature-requests forum channel so ideas do not clog support.
Step 2

Set up the triage bot

The bot auto-replies based on keywords. You can use a simple Discord bot or a webhook integration.

  1. Write the triage function from the minimal example above.
  2. Integrate it with a Discord bot (discord.js, discord.py, or a no-code bot like Ticket Tool).
  3. Configure the bot to auto-reply with the matching bucket template.
  4. If a ticket lacks a transaction ID, the bot closes it with a message to reopen with the ID.
Step 3

Track resolution metrics

You cannot improve what you do not measure.

  1. Log every ticket: date, bucket, resolution time, and whether it was a bug or user error.
  2. After 30 days, review the data. If 70% of tickets are their-fix, improve your documentation.
  3. If your-fix tickets take more than 2 hours per week, your price is too low for the support load.
  4. Adjust your price or your documentation based on the data, not on gut feeling.
Recap

Three tests, in order

Run each test to confirm the workflow works.

  1. Test 1, template enforcement.
    • Action: post a ticket without a transaction ID.
    • Expected: the bot closes the ticket with a message to include the ID.
    • How to verify: the ticket is closed within 60 seconds of posting.
  2. Test 2, triage accuracy.
    • Action: post a ticket saying "how do I change the heal amount" with a valid transaction ID.
    • Expected: the bot triages it as SELF_SERVE and replies with the docs link.
    • How to verify: the reply links to the correct config section in your documentation.
  3. Test 3, their-fix routing.
    • Action: post a ticket with a valid transaction ID and an error caused by a missing convar.
    • Expected: the bot triages it as THEIR_FIX and replies with the config setup instructions.
    • How to verify: the reply asks the customer to check their server.cfg before reopening.
What if the triage bot misclassifies a real bug as self-serve?

The customer replies "that didn't help." The bot detects the follow-up, escalates to YOUR_FIX, and pings you. Manual override exists alongside automation.

The rules you just followed

  • Every support ticket requires a transaction ID before a human reads it.
  • Tickets are triaged into three buckets: self-serve, your-fix, their-fix.
  • Self-serve tickets are answered with documentation links, not custom explanations.
  • Feature requests are routed to a separate channel, not the support queue.
  • Resolution time is tracked so you know when support costs exceed the script price.
